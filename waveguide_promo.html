   Jacob Trombetta has just finished an album called Waveguides.  It's a suburban revenge album inspired by the town where he grew up, Mingo Junction, Ohio.  Mingo is the same town where the steel mill scenes in The Deer Hunter were filmed.  Trombetta spent a number of years touring with Six Parts Seven and Richard Buckner.  Afterwards he went back to school and got a masters degree in Applied Mathematics.  Currently, he is working in the machine vision field in Akron, Ohio, and curate the works and lectures found on Discrete Spectrum.  For more information, please email: jacobtrombetta[at]gmail[dot]com.
